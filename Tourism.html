Welcome to Agoo, La Union

About Agoo

Established in 1578, Agoo is considered the oldest town in La Union and was formerly part of Pangasinan. The town’s name is believed to have come from the pine-like “aroo” trees that once lined its riverbanks. Agoo played a significant role in early evangelization efforts in Northern Luzon, with Franciscan missionaries founding a church dedicated to St. Francis of Assisi. Over time, other religious orders such as the Augustinians contributed to the community’s development by introducing education, religious practices, and construction techniques.

Mission: Promote sustainable tourism.

Vision: Make Agoo a top tourist destination in La Union.
